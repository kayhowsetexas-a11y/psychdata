About Me

I am a first-generation college student, wife, mother, and aspiring behavioral health leader pursuing a Bachelor's degree in Psychology. Currently working as a Crisis Intervention Counselor, I support people through vulnerable moments and use that experience to build compassionate systems for care.

Working in crisis intervention changed my perspective by showing me how urgent mental health needs are, especially for people who feel unheard. This is why I want to use data and operations expertise to improve systems, reduce disparities, and help teams deliver better support.

My journey

As a first-generation college student, I navigate academic growth, family obligations, and a deep desire to make a meaningful impact in behavioral health.

My motivation

My work is rooted in compassion, faith, and a commitment to help others feel seen, heard, and supported during critical moments.
